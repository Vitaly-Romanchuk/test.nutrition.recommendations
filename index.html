Nutrition Recommendations
for Athletes

General Principles

  • 4–5 meals per day
  • Each meal should include complex carbs + protein + vegetables + healthy fats
  • Complex carbohydrates: 90–150 g (dry weight) depending on the goal (mass gain or fat loss).
  • Daily water intake: 30–40 ml per kg of body weight.
  • Balance macros according to your goal: higher carbs for mass gain, lower for fat loss.

Meal Structure

Breakfast

  • Complex carbs: oatmeal, buckwheat, bulgur, quinoa (25–40 g).
  • Protein: 2 boiled eggs or 90–100 g fish/meat/shrimp.
  • Vegetables: 100–200 g fresh or baked.
  • Healthy fats: 1 tsp olive oil, avocado, or a handful of nuts.
  • Optional: berries (50–100 g).

Snack

  • Nuts (20–30 g).
  • Dates (2–3 pcs) or banana.
  • Optional: protein shake or unsweetened yogurt.

Lunch

  • Complex carbs: rice basmati, durum wheat pasta, buckwheat, sweet potato (30–40 g).
  • Protein: 100–120 g fish/meat/shrimp.
  • Vegetables: 150–200 g.
  • Healthy fats: 1 tsp olive oil or flax/chia seeds.
  • Fresh herbs.

Pre-Workout

  • Fast carbs: banana or berries.
  • Small portion of protein (protein bar, cottage cheese).
  • Optional: coffee or matcha for energy.

Dinner

  • Complex carbs: buckwheat, rice, sweet potato (20–30 g, or skip during fat loss).
  • Protein: 90–100 g fish/chicken/turkey/eggs.
  • Vegetables: 150–200 g (light ones: cucumber, zucchini, salad).
  • Healthy fats: olive oil, nuts, avocado.

Post-Workout

  • Protein + fast carbs (protein shake + banana, or cottage cheese with berries).

Additional Tips

  • Red meat (beef, veal) once a week to replenish iron
  • Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel) 2–3 times a week for omega-3.
  • Alternate vegetables of different colors for vitamins and antioxidants.
  • Shift carbs earlier in the day and post-workout on heavy training days.
  • Use spices and herbs (turmeric, rosemary, thyme) to boost recovery.

Supplements (only with doctor’s approval)

  • High-quality Omega-3.
  • Magnesium.
  • Vitamin D + K2.
  • Electrolytes (to support hydration and energy balance).

Hydration

It is very important to drink enough water during the day. Athletes should drink 30–40 ml of water per kg of body weight daily. Electrolytes are recommended to maintain fluid balance and energy.
